General Solution

All Mental Health Issues are treatable in some way. They all vary, but it comes down to whether or not one gets the treatment one needs for themselves. These treatments range from meditation to support groups. BUT ALWAYS SEEK PROFESSIONAL HELP!

Depression

15% of adults will experience depression in thier lifetime. Luckily, there are treatments that will help overcome it! It can be as simple as eating healthy, exercising, or even just getting enough sleep. Of course, these tasks may not cure it fully, but it is a step to getting over it. Another idea is to simply just get help. You aren't alone and there are tons of people and organizations that will help you and fight through this.

ADHD

Unfortunately, ADHD has no known cure at the moment, but there are many options that can help manage this. Small steps like taking a social skills class or having a planner can help proritize things for everday life. If there aren't many options left, there is always medication to treat ADHD.

Anxiety

Everyone has anxiety, altough others may have anxiety that is unstable. Therapy is the main option to overcome and control this. Therapy practices understanding your emotions, which can help develop coping skills. Not only that, therapy is a safe and comfortable environment so people with anxiety can feel safe.

Self Harm

Self harm always comes with a reason, and the reason can be becuase of many things. An example like stress may be a root of it, and a soultion to this is releasing that stress in a healthy act. Find a different way to release your emotions in a benefitual way. You can release stress by gasing at a calming river. You can release rage by exercising. There are many healthy options to cope with whatever a person is going through, the person just has to find the right act for them.

PSTD

Post-traumatic stress disorder, PTSD, is caused by a trauma that occured in the past. PTSD therapy has 3 main goals to treat this: improve symptoms, teach skills to deal with it, and restore self-esteem. Going to therapy can help change thought patterns that are disturbing your life.

Antisocial personality disorder

The goal for treatment of ASPD is to be able to control emotions and feelings. Psychotherapy, also called talk therapy, is used to treat antisocial personality disorder. This therapy includes controling anger and focuses on changing a person's thinking. Another treatment is medication, although there can be symptoms. Because of this, medication should only be the option when things get out of hand.
