Why Mental Health Is Important

Emotional and mental health is important because it’s a vital part of your life and impacts your thoughts, behaviors and emotions. Being healthy emotionally can promote productivity and effectiveness in activities such as work, school, and life in general.

STIGMA: The current stigma towards mental health is much better than what it was years ago. Often times people suffering from such illnesses are regarded as going through "a phase" or that these things would not happen if they tried. This has lead many people suffering from these issues to consider their illnesses to be non-exsistant and has lead to further damage.

Our Goal: By providing a educational source on different types of Mental Health Issues we can shed some light on how common some of these issues are and share some basic solutions and resources to help those affected.

Mental Health Statistics:

  • One in 5 adults experiences a mental health condition every year.
  • Nearly 1 in 25 (10 million) adults in America live with a serious mental illness.
  • 6.9% of adults in the U.S.—16 million—had at least one major depressive episode in the past year.
  • One-half of all chronic mental illness begins by the age of 14; three-quarters by the age of 24.
